About Apes

Apes are a group of primates known for their intelligence and similarity to humans. They belong to the family Hominidae and are divided into two main groups: great apes and lesser apes.

Types of Apes

Great apes include gorillas, chimpanzees, bonobos, and orangutans. These apes are known for their large size, strength, and complex social behaviors.

Lesser apes, such as gibbons and siamangs, are smaller in size and are known for their agility and acrobatic abilities in the treetops.

Characteristics

Apes are characterized by their lack of a tail, large brains relative to body size, and the ability to use tools. They are omnivorous, with diets that include fruits, leaves, insects, and occasionally small animals.

Conservation

Many ape species are endangered due to habitat loss and poaching. Conservation efforts are underway to protect these remarkable animals and their habitats.
